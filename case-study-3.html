Users were struggling to scan QR codes on Hangry receipts, flooding support with complaints. I led the research and design of a contextual in-app help system that reduced those complaints by 54% within three months of launch.

01

Problem

Users struggled with scanning QR codes on Hangry receipts, resulting in low success rates and a high volume of support complaints. The friction was causing users to abandon the loyalty flow entirely, hurting both retention and satisfaction.

Problem — complaint data / support volume
02

Research

We ran controlled experiments to identify the root causes, testing hypotheses around printer quality, QR code density, and device camera capabilities across iOS and Android devices.

Research — experiment setup / testing process
Finding 01
Flattening receipts dramatically improved scan success rates
Finding 02
Print area size had no meaningful impact on performance
Finding 03
iOS performed better than Android on bent or damaged receipts
Finding 04
The Hangry app camera outperformed native camera alternatives
03

Competitive Benchmarking

I analyzed how other apps handle similar scanning flows. Only Dana and Grab provided both feature explanations and a dedicated help section with visual instructions — a clear gap in the market that validated our solution direction.

Benchmarking — Dana & Grab comparison
04

Solution

We iterated through two approaches before landing on the final solution.

❌ First Iteration — Rejected
Mandatory pre-scan instructions
Blocked users before scanning, adding friction to the happy path. Rejected due to potential drop-off for users who didn't need help.
✓ Final Implementation — Shipped
Contextual help system — user-initiated
A help option available during scanning, not before. Step-by-step instructions addressing specific failure scenarios: folded receipts, water damage, poor lighting. Includes a clear CTA to re-scan after reading.
Solution — final UI screens
05

Results

−54%
Reduction in user complaints related to QR scanning, measured over a 3-month period following feature launch.

Three-month comparative data confirmed a 54% drop in QR-related support complaints. The contextual help approach proved that giving users the right information at the right moment — without forcing it on them — was the key to improving success rates without hurting the core scanning experience.
